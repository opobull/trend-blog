Netflix's biggest comedy hit of early 2025. **Cameron Diaz** and **Jamie Foxx** play a married couple who left the spy life behind to raise their kids in the suburbs ‚Äî only to be dragged back into international espionage when their cover is blown.

- **Best for**: Eddie Murphy fans, 80s action-comedy nostalgia

## üòÇ Absurdist & Cult Comedies

### 4. Between Two Ferns: The Movie (2019)

Zach Galifianakis expands his cult web series ‚Äî where he interviews celebrities in the most deliberately awkward fashion possible ‚Äî into a full narrative film. Forced to produce 10 celebrity interviews for his employer, he travels the country conducting excruciatingly uncomfortable sit-downs with Benedict Cumberbatch, Brie Larson, Paul Rudd, Keanu Reeves, and many more.

**Between Two Ferns** is exactly the right length for its premise and commits fully to the bit. Galifianakis' deadpan hostility toward his famous guests never gets old.

- **Runtime**: 82 minutes
- **Best for**: Cringe comedy fans, people who watched the web series, everyone who finds awkward silence funny

### 5. Best in Show (2000)

Christopher Guest's masterpiece of improvised mockumentary comedy. A disparate group of dog owners converge on the Mayflower Kennel Club Dog Show in Philadelphia, each competing with pathological intensity.

The ensemble ‚Äî Eugene Levy, Catherine O'Hara, Parker Posey, Jennifer Coolidge, Fred Willard, Bob Balaban ‚Äî is operating at the highest level of comedic craft. Fred Willard's color commentary is some of the funniest work in film history. **Best in Show** rewards multiple rewatches; you notice new jokes every time.

- **Runtime**: 90 minutes
- **Best for**: Comedy connoisseurs, fans of improvised performance, dog owners

### 6. This Is Spinal Tap (1984)

The original mockumentary. Rob Reiner follows the fictional British heavy metal band Spinal Tap on their disastrous American comeback tour. The jokes are as funny now as they were in 1984 ‚Äî the tiny Stonehenge, the amplifier that "goes to 11," the rotating members who die in "bizarre gardening accidents."

**This Is Spinal Tap** invented a genre that Christopher Guest, The Office, and countless others built careers on.

- **Runtime**: 82 minutes
- **Best for**: Music fans, comedy history enthusiasts, anyone who hasn't seen the most influential comedy film of the past 40 years

### 7. The Grand Budapest Hotel (2014)

Wes Anderson's mystery-comedy-adventure set in a fictional European republic between the wars. A legendary concierge (Ralph Fiennes) and his lobby boy Zero Moustafa become entangled in a murder, a stolen painting, and a vast inheritance dispute.

**The Grand Budapest Hotel** is meticulously crafted comedy ‚Äî every frame composed like a painting, every line delivered with precise comic timing. Fiennes has never been funnier. **Won 4 Academy Awards.**

- **Starring**: Ralph Fiennes, Tony Revolori, Tilda Swinton, Jude Law, F. Murray Abraham
- **Runtime**: 100 minutes
- **Best for**: Fans of precise, visual comedy; Wes Anderson enthusiasts

### 12. The Out-Laws (2023)

Adam Devine plays a mild-mannered bank manager who suspects his future in-laws (Pierce Brosnan, Ellen Barkin) are the infamous "Out-Laws" bank robbers. Adam Sandler and Jennifer Aniston as a New York couple who find themselves framed for the murder of a billionaire on a Mediterranean yacht ‚Äî then kidnapping at a Parisian wedding in the sequel. Both are breezy, globe-trotting comedies that don't demand anything from the viewer.

**Murder Mystery 2** became one of Netflix's most-watched films in its debut weekend. Sometimes that's exactly what you need.

- **Starring**: Adam Sandler, Jennifer Aniston
- **Runtime**: ~96 minutes each
- **Best for**: Easy entertainment, Sandler-Aniston fan group watches

### 14. The Nice Guys (2016)

1970s Los Angeles. A bumbling private detective (Ryan Gosling) and a hired enforcer (Russell Crowe) form an unlikely partnership to find a missing girl connected to the death of a porn star. **The Nice Guys** is one of the most underappreciated comedy-thrillers of recent years ‚Äî the Gosling-Crowe dynamic is extraordinary, the period detail is perfect, and the comedy is consistently sharp.

- **Starring**: Ryan Gosling, Russell Crowe
- **Runtime**: 116 minutes
- **Best for**: Anyone who likes their comedy with craft and character

### 15. Game Night (2018)

A group of friends' regular game night takes a surreal turn when a murder mystery game becomes real. **Game Night** is relentlessly inventive ‚Äî the cinematography mimics board game photography (objects shot to look miniature), and the escalating confusion between game and reality generates sustained comic momentum.

- **Starring**: Jason Bateman, Rachel McAdams, Kyle Chandler
- **Runtime**: 100 minutes
- **Best for**: Game enthusiasts, group movie nights, people who like comedies with a genuine concept
