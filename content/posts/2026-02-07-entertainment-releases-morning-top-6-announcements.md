## 1. "Bring Her Back" Sweeps 2026 AACTA Awards with 10 Wins

![Entertainment Morning Roundup](/images/entertainment-morning-2026-02-07.jpg)

**A24's** horror-thriller **Bring Her Back** dominated the 2026 AACTA (Australian Academy of Cinema and Television Arts) Awards, winning 10 of its 16 nominations. The film, directed by Adelaide brothers **Danny and Michael Philippou** (Talk to Me), marks a major milestone for the duo, surpassing their previous record of eight awards for their 2024 global breakout.

Academy Award nominee **Sally Hawkins** (The Shape of Water) won **Best Lead Actress in Film** for her portrayal of a sinister foster mother in this supernatural horror about orphans fostered by a member of the occult.

**Key Wins:**

| Category | Winner |
|----------|--------|
| Best Film | Bring Her Back |
| Best Direction | Danny & Michael Philippou |
| Best Lead Actress | Sally Hawkins |
| Best Cinematography | Bring Her Back |
| Best Editing | Bring Her Back |
| Best Original Score | Bring Her Back |
| Best Sound | Bring Her Back |
| Best Costume Design | Bring Her Back |
| Best Hair and Makeup | Bring Her Back |
| Best Casting | Bring Her Back |

**Jacob Elordi** also took home **Best Lead Actor in a Drama** for his portrayal of army surgeon Dorrigo Evans in **The Narrow Road to the Deep North**, based on Richard Flanagan's Booker Prize-winning novel. Elordi is currently on a publicity tour for the upcoming **Wuthering Heights** with co-star **Margot Robbie**, who won Favourite Australian Actress.

## 3. "Bridgerton" Season 4 Part 1 Dominates Netflix Weekly Charts

**Netflix's** period drama **Bridgerton** continues its reign over streaming with Season 4 Part 1 topping the weekly TV charts. The first four episodes drew **39.7 million views** over the premiere weekend (January 29), making it by far the most-watched title on the platform from January 26 to February 1.

**Season 4** focuses on **Benedict Bridgerton** (Luke Thompson) and his romance with **Sophie Baek** (Yerin Ha), a mysterious woman with a complicated past. The season has already sparked intense fan discussions, particularly around a potential major character death teased for Part 2.

**Viewership Comparison:**

| Season | First Weekend Views |
|--------|---------------------|
| Season 3 Part 1 | 45.1M |
| Season 4 Part 1 | 39.7M |

While slightly down from Season 3's premiere, Season 4 remains a strong performer with a healthy chance of joining its predecessors on Netflix's Most Popular list. **Part 2** is scheduled to premiere on **February 26, 2026**, with the final four episodes.

## 4. "The Lincoln Lawyer" Renewed for Season 5 After Strong Season 4 Premiere

**Netflix** announced that legal drama **The Lincoln Lawyer** has been renewed for a **fifth season**, just one week before Season 4's February 5 premiere. The 10-episode fourth season sees defense attorney **Mickey Haller** (Manuel Garcia-Rulfo) on the other side of the courtroom after being framed for murder.

**Season 4 Highlights:**
- Mickey arrested after a body is discovered in his car trunk
- New cast additions: **Cobie Smulders**, **Sasha Alexander**, **Constance Zimmer**
- Death of one of Mickey's closest confidants
- Introduction of a mysterious new character with major implications

"Season 4 is the most challenging and intensely personal journey we've taken Mickey Haller on yet," co-showrunners **Ted Humphrey** and **Dailyn Rodriguez** said in a statement. "We're thrilled and grateful to be able to continue the ride in Season 5."

**Season 5** will consist of 10 episodes based on Michael Connelly's seventh Lincoln Lawyer novel, **Resurrection Walk** (2023), with an expected release in 2027.

## 5. Bad Bunny Previews Super Bowl LX Halftime Show Performance

Puerto Rican superstar **Bad Bunny** shared new details about his upcoming **Apple Music Super Bowl LX Halftime Show** at a press conference in San Francisco on Thursday. The performance, scheduled for **Sunday, February 8**, comes just one week after his historic triple Grammy win, including **Album of the Year** for "DeBÍ TiRAR MáS FOToS"—the first all-Spanish-language album to win the top prize.

"I'm just trying to enjoy it," Bad Bunny said. "I know I'm gonna have fun. All the crew, all the people, they're going to have fun that day."

**Key Details:**

| Item | Information |
|------|-------------|
| Event | Super Bowl LX Halftime Show |
| Date | February 8, 2026 |
| Location | Levi's Stadium, Santa Clara, CA |
| Matchup | Seattle Seahawks vs New England Patriots |
| Sponsor | Apple Music |

When asked about potential guest performers, Bad Bunny smiled and said, "You know that's something that I not gonna tell you. I don't know why you asked that." The artist previously made a guest appearance during Super Bowl LIV with **Jennifer Lopez** and **Shakira** in 2020.

## 6. Jacob Elordi Continues Awards Season Momentum with AACTA Win

Australian actor **Jacob Elordi** continues his impressive awards season run, taking home **Best Lead Actor in a Drama** at the 2026 AACTA Awards for **The Narrow Road to the Deep North**. The win comes fresh off his **Critics' Choice Movie Awards** victory for his portrayal of the Creature in **Guillermo del Toro's Frankenstein**.

**Elordi's 2026 Awards Season:**

| Award | Role | Film/Series |
|-------|------|-------------|
| Critics' Choice Award | The Creature | Frankenstein |
| AACTA Best Lead Actor | Dorrigo Evans | The Narrow Road to the Deep North |
| AACTA Audience Choice | Favourite Australian Actor | - |

Elordi, 28, is now in the running for an **Oscar**, **BAFTA**, and **Screen Actors Guild Award** for his Frankenstein performance. Currently on a publicity tour for the upcoming **Wuthering Heights** (releasing next week), Elordi accepted his AACTA awards via a pre-recorded video link.

The Narrow Road to the Deep North, an adaptation of Richard Flanagan's Booker Prize-winning novel about WWII prisoners of war, won a total of nine AACTA awards from 12 nominations, including Best Supporting Actress for **Heather Mitchell**.
