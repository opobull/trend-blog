## The 40 Best Movies on Max Right Now

### 1. The Godfather (1972)
**Director**: Francis Ford Coppola | **Rating**: 97% on Rotten Tomatoes | **IMDb**: 9.2/10

The second-highest-rated film on IMDb and one of the greatest movies ever made. *The Godfather* follows the Corleone crime family as patriarch Vito (Marlon Brando) passes power to his reluctant son Michael (Al Pacino). What starts as a gangster film becomes a Shakespearean tragedy about power, family, and the corruption of the American Dream.

Every frame is iconic. Every performance is perfect. Every line is quotable. If you've somehow never seen it, Max is the place to start.

**Why it's #1**: The blueprint for modern crime cinema. Literally perfect filmmaking.

**Watch if you like**: Crime sagas, family dramas, Al Pacino, Marlon Brando, American classics

**Follow-up**: *The Godfather Part II* (also on Max) is arguably even better.

---

### 2. The Shawshank Redemption (1994)
**Director**: Frank Darabont | **Rating**: 91% on Rotten Tomatoes | **IMDb**: 9.3/10

The #1 movie on IMDb. Banker Andy Dufresne (Tim Robbins) is wrongly convicted of murder and sent to Shawshank Prison, where he befriends Red (Morgan Freeman) and quietly plans his escape over decades.

*Shawshank* flopped in theaters but became a cultural phenomenon through cable TV and home video. It's the ultimate story of hope, friendship, and resilience — and it's impossible to turn off once you start watching.

**Why it's #2**: The most beloved movie in the world. Morgan Freeman's narration is perfect.

**Watch if you like**: Prison dramas, hope against the odds, Tim Robbins, Morgan Freeman, feel-good endings

---

### 3. The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring (2001)
**Director**: Peter Jackson | **Rating**: 91% on Rotten Tomatoes | **IMDb**: 8.9/10

The first entry in the greatest fantasy trilogy ever made. Frodo Baggins (Elijah Wood) inherits the One Ring and must journey to Mount Doom to destroy it before dark lord Sauron conquers Middle-earth.

Peter Jackson's adaptation of J.R.R. Tolkien's novels is a perfect blend of epic scope, emotional intimacy, and practical craftsmanship. The trilogy won 17 Oscars across three films, including Best Picture for *The Return of the King*.

All three *Lord of the Rings* films are available on Max.

**Why it's #3**: The most engulfing fantasy epic ever made. Changed cinema forever.

**Watch if you like**: Fantasy epics, adventure, world-building, emotional journeys, practical effects

**Watch next**: *The Two Towers* and *The Return of the King* (both on Max)

---

### 4. The Dark Knight (2008)
**Director**: Christopher Nolan | **Rating**: 94% on Rotten Tomatoes | **IMDb**: 9.0/10

Christopher Nolan's Batman sequel transcended the superhero genre and became a crime thriller masterpiece. Heath Ledger's Joker is one of the greatest villain performances in cinema history (he won a posthumous Oscar). Christian Bale's Batman faces moral dilemmas that feel genuinely weighty.

*The Dark Knight* proved superhero films could be prestige cinema. It ranks in IMDb's top 10 films of all time.

**Why it's #4**: The best superhero movie ever made. Heath Ledger's Joker is unforgettable.

**Watch if you like**: Batman, crime thrillers, Christopher Nolan, moral complexity, superhero films

**Also on Max**: *Batman Begins* and *The Dark Knight Rises* (complete the trilogy)

---

### 5. Dune: Part Two (2024)
**Director**: Denis Villeneuve | **Rating**: 92% on Rotten Tomatoes | **IMDb**: 8.6/10

Denis Villeneuve's conclusion to Frank Herbert's *Dune* is a sci-fi epic on the scale of *Lawrence of Arabia*. Paul Atreides (Timothée Chalamet) joins the Fremen to wage war against House Harkonnen while grappling with visions of a terrible future.

*Dune: Part Two* is visually stunning, narratively complex, and earned multiple Oscar nominations. It's the rare sequel that improves on an already-great first film.

**Why it's #5**: The best sci-fi epic since *The Lord of the Rings*. Stunning on every level.

**Watch if you like**: Sci-fi, desert planets, political intrigue, Timothée Chalamet, Zendaya, world-building

**Watch first**: *Dune* (2021, also on Max)

---

### 6. The Godfather Part II (1974)
**Director**: Francis Ford Coppola | **Rating**: 96% on Rotten Tomatoes | **IMDb**: 9.0/10

The rare sequel that matches (or surpasses) the original. *Part II* intercuts Michael Corleone's rise to ruthless power in the 1950s with flashbacks to young Vito's immigrant journey in 1917 New York (Robert De Niro in an Oscar-winning performance).

The film won Best Picture, making it the only sequel to achieve this until *The Lord of the Rings: Return of the King*.

**Why it's #6**: The greatest sequel ever made. De Niro and Pacino both at their peak.

**Watch if you like**: *The Godfather*, crime sagas, dual timelines, immigrant stories

---

### 7. The Substance (2024)
**Director**: Coralie Fargeat | **Rating**: 91% on Rotten Tomatoes | **Oscar**: 5 nominations including Best Picture

Demi Moore plays an aging Hollywood star who takes an experimental drug to regain her youth — with horrifying consequences. *The Substance* is body horror with a sharp satirical edge, critiquing Hollywood's obsession with youth and beauty standards.

The film won Best Makeup at the 2026 Oscars and is nominated for Best Picture. Demi Moore's performance is career-best, and the film's visual style is unforgettable.

**Why it's #7**: The best horror film of 2024. Demi Moore's comeback performance.

**Watch if you like**: Body horror, satire, *Requiem for a Dream*, *Black Swan*, Hollywood critique

**Warning**: Very graphic. Not for the squeamish.

---

### 8. Spirited Away (2001)
**Director**: Hayao Miyazaki | **Rating**: 97% on Rotten Tomatoes | **Oscar**: Best Animated Feature

A young girl named Chihiro stumbles into a magical bathhouse for spirits and must work there to save her parents, who've been turned into pigs. Hayao Miyazaki's masterpiece is the best place to start with Studio Ghibli — whimsical, emotional, and visually stunning.

*Spirited Away* is the highest-grossing film in Japanese history and won the Oscar for Best Animated Feature in 2003.

**Why it's #8**: The best animated film ever made. Perfect for all ages.

**Watch if you like**: Studio Ghibli, animation, fantasy, coming-of-age, Japanese cinema

**Also on Max**: Nearly the entire Studio Ghibli catalog

### 9. Sinners (2024)
**Director**: Ryan Coogler | **Rating**: 89% on Rotten Tomatoes | **Oscar**: Best Picture nominee

Ryan Coogler's latest film (following *Black Panther: Wakanda Forever*) is a period drama set in the Jim Crow South. The film is nominated for Best Picture at the 2026 Oscars and features powerful performances addressing systemic racism and resilience.

Details on the plot are being kept under wraps, but early reviews call it Coogler's most ambitious and personal work.

**Why it's #9**: 2026 Best Picture nominee. Ryan Coogler's masterpiece.

**Watch if you like**: Period dramas, Ryan Coogler, social justice films, Oscar nominees

### 11. Goodfellas (1990)
**Director**: Martin Scorsese | **Rating**: 96% on Rotten Tomatoes | **IMDb**: 8.7/10

Martin Scorsese's mob masterpiece follows Henry Hill (Ray Liotta) as he rises through the ranks of the Italian-American mafia. The film is kinetic, violent, darkly funny, and features career-best performances from Liotta, Robert De Niro, and Joe Pesci (who won an Oscar).

**Why it's #11**: The most rewatchable crime film ever made. Joe Pesci is terrifying.

**Watch if you like**: Mob movies, Scorsese, *The Godfather*, true crime, fast-paced editing

---

### 12. The Zone of Interest (2023)
**Director**: Jonathan Glazer | **Rating**: 92% on Rotten Tomatoes | **Oscar**: Best International Feature winner, Best Picture nominee

A deeply unsettling Holocaust film that never shows the atrocities directly. Instead, it follows Rudolf Höss, the commandant of Auschwitz, and his family living in a beautiful home next to the concentration camp. The horrors occur just off-screen, creating unbearable tension.

The film won Best International Feature at the 2024 Oscars.

**Why it's #12**: The most important Holocaust film since *Schindler's List*. Devastating and necessary.

**Watch if you like**: Historical dramas, Holocaust films, arthouse cinema, challenging narratives

---

### 13. Pulp Fiction (1994)
**Director**: Quentin Tarantino | **Rating**: 92% on Rotten Tomatoes | **Oscar**: Best Original Screenplay

Tarantino's masterpiece interweaves multiple crime stories in non-linear fashion. Hitmen, boxers, gangsters, and a mysterious briefcase all collide in Los Angeles. The dialogue is iconic, the soundtrack is perfect, and the performances (John Travolta, Uma Thurman, Samuel L. Jackson) are legendary.

**Why it's #13**: Tarantino's best film. Changed how movies tell stories.

**Watch if you like**: Non-linear narratives, crime films, Tarantino, quotable dialogue

---

### 14. 2001: A Space Odyssey (1968)
**Director**: Stanley Kubrick | **Rating**: 92% on Rotten Tomatoes | **IMDb**: 8.3/10

Stanley Kubrick's sci-fi epic follows humanity's evolution from apes to space travelers, with a mysterious black monolith guiding the journey. The film is slow, meditative, and visually groundbreaking — it looks better than most modern sci-fi 50+ years later.

**Why it's #14**: The most visually stunning sci-fi film ever made. Still relevant.

**Watch if you like**: Hard sci-fi, Stanley Kubrick, slow burns, philosophical films, HAL 9000

---

### 15. The Silence of the Lambs (1991)
**Director**: Jonathan Demme | **Rating**: 95% on Rotten Tomatoes | **Oscar**: Big Five winner (Picture, Director, Actor, Actress, Screenplay)

FBI trainee Clarice Starling (Jodie Foster) must consult imprisoned cannibal Dr. Hannibal Lecter (Anthony Hopkins) to catch another serial killer. *The Silence of the Lambs* is one of only three films to win all five major Oscars.

Hopkins' Lecter is one of cinema's greatest villains — and he's only on screen for 16 minutes.

**Why it's #15**: The best thriller of the 1990s. Anthony Hopkins is iconic.

**Watch if you like**: Thrillers, serial killers, FBI procedurals, Jodie Foster, Anthony Hopkins

---

### 16. Schindler's List (1993)
**Director**: Steven Spielberg | **Rating**: 98% on Rotten Tomatoes | **Oscar**: 7 wins including Best Picture

Steven Spielberg's Holocaust drama follows Oskar Schindler (Liam Neeson), a German businessman who saved over 1,000 Jewish lives during WWII. The film is shot in black-and-white and features one of cinema's most devastating endings.

**Why it's #16**: The definitive Holocaust film. Essential viewing.

**Watch if you like**: Historical dramas, Spielberg, WWII films, true stories, Liam Neeson

---

### 17. Mad Max: Fury Road (2015)
**Director**: George Miller | **Rating**: 97% on Rotten Tomatoes | **Oscar**: 6 wins (most of the night)

A two-hour desert car chase with minimal dialogue and maximum adrenaline. Imperator Furiosa (Charlize Theron) and Max (Tom Hardy) escape a tyrannical warlord in a post-apocalyptic wasteland. *Fury Road* won 6 Oscars and redefined action filmmaking.

**Why it's #17**: The best action film of the 2010s. Non-stop spectacle.

**Watch if you like**: Action, car chases, dystopian futures, Charlize Theron, practical effects

---

### 18. Casablanca (1942)
**Director**: Michael Curtiz | **Rating**: 99% on Rotten Tomatoes | **Oscar**: Best Picture winner

Rick Blaine (Humphrey Bogart) runs a nightclub in Casablanca during WWII. When his ex-lover Ilsa (Ingrid Bergman) walks in with her Resistance leader husband, Rick must choose between love and duty.

*Casablanca* is the most romantic film ever made. "Here's looking at you, kid" remains cinema's most iconic line.

**Why it's #18**: Old Hollywood perfection. Endlessly quotable.

**Watch if you like**: Classic Hollywood, romance, WWII films, Humphrey Bogart, black-and-white cinema

---

### 19. The Matrix (1999)
**Directors**: The Wachowskis | **Rating**: 88% on Rotten Tomatoes | **Oscar**: 4 wins

Neo (Keanu Reeves) discovers reality is a simulation controlled by machines. *The Matrix* revolutionized action cinema with its "bullet time" effects and philosophical depth. The leather coats, wire-fu fights, and green tint became iconic.

**Why it's #19**: Changed action cinema forever. Still looks incredible.

**Watch if you like**: Sci-fi, cyberpunk, philosophy, Keanu Reeves, kung fu

**Warning**: Skip the sequels unless you're a completist.

### 20. Inception (2010)
**Director**: Christopher Nolan | **Rating**: 87% on Rotten Tomatoes | **Oscar**: 4 wins

Dom Cobb (Leonardo DiCaprio) is a thief who steals secrets from people's dreams. His final job: plant an idea in someone's mind (inception). Nolan's mind-bending heist film features dreams within dreams within dreams — and an ending that still sparks debate.

**Why it's#20**: The smartest blockbuster of the 2010s. That ending.

**Watch if you like**: Christopher Nolan, heist films, dream logic, Leonardo DiCaprio, puzzle-box movies

---

### 21. Interstellar (2014)
**Director**: Christopher Nolan | **Rating**: 73% on Rotten Tomatoes | **IMDb**: 8.7/10

A farmer-turned-astronaut (Matthew McConaughey) travels through a wormhole to find a new home for humanity. *Interstellar* is Nolan's most emotional film, blending hard sci-fi with father-daughter drama. The black hole visuals are scientifically accurate and breathtaking.

**Why it's #21**: The most emotionally powerful sci-fi film since *2001*. Hans Zimmer's score is legendary.

**Watch if you like**: Space exploration, hard sci-fi, Matthew McConaughey, Christopher Nolan, crying at movies

### 22. The Departed (2006)
**Director**: Martin Scorsese | **Rating**: 90% on Rotten Tomatoes | **Oscar**: Best Picture winner

An undercover cop (Leonardo DiCaprio) infiltrates the mob while a mob mole (Matt Damon) infiltrates the police. Scorsese's Boston crime thriller is tense, violent, and features an all-timer cast (Jack Nicholson, Mark Wahlberg, Martin Sheen).

**Why it's #22**: Scorsese finally won Best Director. Leo and Matt Damon at their best.

**Watch if you like**: Crime thrillers, undercover cops, Boston accents, Scorsese, twists

### 23. Blade Runner 2049 (2017)
**Director**: Denis Villeneuve | **Rating**: 88% on Rotten Tomatoes | **Oscar**: 2 wins

Denis Villeneuve's sequel to the 1982 sci-fi classic follows a new Blade Runner (Ryan Gosling) uncovering a secret that could collapse society. The film is a slow, meditative masterpiece with the most beautiful cinematography of the 2010s.

**Why it's #23**: The best-looking film on Max. Roger Deakins' cinematography won an Oscar.

**Watch if you like**: Sci-fi, cyberpunk, slow burns, Ryan Gosling, Harrison Ford, visual poetry

**Watch first**: *Blade Runner* (1982, also on Max)

### 24. The Green Mile (1999)
**Director**: Frank Darabont | **Rating**: 79% on Rotten Tomatoes | **IMDb**: 8.6/10

A death row prison guard (Tom Hanks) encounters a gentle giant inmate (Michael Clarke Duncan) with supernatural healing powers. From the director of *The Shawshank Redemption*, *The Green Mile* is an emotional gut-punch about mercy, justice, and miracles.

**Why it's #24**: The most emotionally devastating prison film ever made. Bring tissues.

**Watch if you like**: *Shawshank*, prison dramas, Tom Hanks, Stephen King adaptations, crying

### 25. No Country for Old Men (2007)
**Directors**: Coen Brothers | **Rating**: 93% on Rotten Tomatoes | **Oscar**: Best Picture winner

A hunter (Josh Brolin) stumbles upon a drug deal gone wrong and takes $2 million, pursued by psychopathic hitman Anton Chigurh (Javier Bardem). The Coen Brothers' neo-Western is bleak, violent, and features one of cinema's most terrifying villains.

**Why it's #25**: Javier Bardem's Chigurh is unforgettable. Coen Brothers at their best.

**Watch if you like**: Westerns, cat-and-mouse thrillers, Coen Brothers, Javier Bardem, bleakness

### 26. There Will Be Blood (2007)
**Director**: Paul Thomas Anderson | **Rating**: 91% on Rotten Tomatoes | **Oscar**: 2 wins

Daniel Plainview (Daniel Day-Lewis) is an oil prospector whose greed and ambition consume him. Paul Thomas Anderson's epic character study is a meditation on capitalism, religion, and American ambition. Day-Lewis won his second Best Actor Oscar.

**Why it's #26**: Daniel Day-Lewis' greatest performance. "I drink your milkshake!"

**Watch if you like**: Character studies, period dramas, Daniel Day-Lewis, capitalism critique, slow burns

**Director**: Christopher Nolan | **Rating**: 76% on Rotten Tomatoes | **IMDb**: 8.5/10

Rival magicians (Christian Bale, Hugh Jackman) engage in an escalating battle of one-upmanship. Nolan's twisty thriller is about obsession, sacrifice, and the cost of perfection. The ending recontextualizes everything.

**Why it's #27**: Nolan's most underrated film. Deserves multiple viewings.

**Watch if you like**: Magicians, twist endings, Christian Bale, Hugh Jackman, puzzle-box films
