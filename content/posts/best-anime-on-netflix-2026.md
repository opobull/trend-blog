## The 25 Best Anime on Netflix (2026)

### üî• Action & Shonen

#### 1. Jujutsu Kaisen (Seasons 1‚Äì2)

If you can only watch one anime on this list, make it **Jujutsu Kaisen**. This supernatural action series follows **Yuji Itadori**, a high school student who swallows a cursed finger belonging to the legendary demon Ryomen Sukuna ‚Äî and becomes his host.

What follows is one of the most thrilling rides in modern anime. The animation quality is genuinely jaw-dropping, the fight choreography is some of the best ever put to screen, and the characters are instantly lovable. Season 2 raised the stakes even higher with the Shibuya Incident arc ‚Äî a sequence of events that anime fans are still talking about.

- **Genre**: Supernatural action, shonen
- **Episodes**: 47+ across two seasons
- **Best for**: Action fans, fans of My Hero Academia or Naruto

#### 2. Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba

**Demon Slayer** is a visual masterpiece. Studio Ufotable's animation ‚Äî all fluid motion, water-like sword strikes, and vivid color ‚Äî makes even the quiet moments beautiful. The story follows **Tanjiro Kamado**, a boy who becomes a Demon Slayer after his family is slaughtered and his sister transformed into a demon.

All four seasons are on Netflix, and Season 4 (the Hashira Training arc) recently dropped. The upcoming movie trilogy **Demon Slayer: Infinity Castle** will adapt the final arc, so now is the perfect time to catch up.

- **Genre**: Action/Dark Fantasy
- **Episodes**: 55+ plus films
- **Best for**: Viewers who want stunning animation and emotional storytelling

#### 3. Chainsaw Man

**Chainsaw Man** is not your average shonen. Based on Tatsuki Fujimoto's manga, the series follows **Denji**, a teenager who merges with his pet devil-dog Pochita to become a chainsaw-powered demon hunter. He's broke, orphaned, and just wants to eat a nice meal and sleep in a real bed.

The series blends workplace comedy with ultra-violent action in a way that feels completely fresh. The animation from MAPPA is stunning, and the film adaptation ‚Äî **Chainsaw Man ‚Äì The Movie: Reze Arc** ‚Äî was one of the biggest anime theatrical releases of 2025.

- **Genre**: Dark action, dark comedy
- **Episodes**: 12 (Season 1)
- **Best for**: Fans of irreverent, adult-oriented action anime

#### 4. One Piece

Yes, **One Piece** has over 1,100 episodes. No, you don't need to watch them all. But once you fall in love with **Monkey D. Luffy** and his crew, each arc pulls you deeper into one of the most ambitious stories in all of fiction.

Netflix has a significant chunk of the series, and the ongoing **Egghead Island arc** (currently airing) is considered by many fans to be the best content in the entire series. If the episode count is daunting, start at episode 517 (the Fishman Island arc, after the timeskip) for a clean entry point.

- **Genre**: Action/Adventure
- **Episodes**: 1,100+ (partial selection on Netflix)
- **Best for**: Fans willing to commit to an epic long-running saga

### üå∏ Fantasy & Emotional

#### 5. Frieren: Beyond Journey's End

**Frieren** is a once-in-a-generation anime. Following an elven mage who outlives the human heroes she adventured with, the series explores **grief, memory, and what it means to connect** with people whose lives are so much shorter than your own.

The animation is breathtaking, the writing is deeply thoughtful, and the final act delivers action sequences that rival anything in the shonen genre. Frieren is currently rated as one of the highest-rated anime of all time on MyAnimeList.

- **Genre**: Fantasy, slice of life, emotional drama
- **Episodes**: 28
- **Best for**: Viewers who want depth and emotional resonance alongside beautiful animation

#### 6. Violet Evergarden

**Violet Evergarden** is one of the most visually stunning anime ever produced. Following a former child soldier who becomes a letter-writer in a post-war world, the series deals with **trauma, emotional growth, and the power of human connection** in ways that will genuinely move you to tears.

Produced by Kyoto Animation, each episode is a standalone story that slowly builds Violet's understanding of human emotion. The feature film **Violet Evergarden: The Movie** is also available and serves as a perfect conclusion.

- **Genre**: Drama, fantasy, slice of life
- **Episodes**: 13 + specials + film
- **Best for**: Anyone who wants a beautifully crafted emotional journey

#### 7. A Silent Voice (Movie)

**A Silent Voice** (*Koe no Katachi*) is one of the finest anime films ever made. The story follows **Shoya**, a former bully who tormented a deaf classmate named Shoko in elementary school, and his attempt to make amends years later.

This is a film about bullying, guilt, redemption, and the difficulty of forgiving yourself. It handles heavy subject matter with tremendous sensitivity and care. The animation is exquisite throughout.

- **Genre**: Drama, romance
- **Length**: Film (~130 minutes)
- **Best for**: Viewers who want a profound standalone anime experience

### ü§ñ Sci-Fi & Mecha

#### 8. Neon Genesis Evangelion

**Neon Genesis Evangelion** changed anime forever. Released in 1995, this mecha series begins as a fairly conventional story about teenagers piloting giant robots to fight alien threats ‚Äî then slowly, brilliantly deconstructs everything you thought you knew about the genre.

The final episodes of the original series are among the most discussed, analyzed, and debated episodes in anime history. The **End of Evangelion** film is also on Netflix. If you've never watched Eva, this is essential viewing.

- **Genre**: Mecha, psychological, sci-fi
- **Episodes**: 26 + film
- **Best for**: Viewers who want anime that challenges and provokes as much as it entertains

#### 9. Cyberpunk: Edgerunners

Set in the world of **Cyberpunk 2077**, **Edgerunners** is a 10-episode Netflix Original produced by Studio Trigger. Following **David Martinez**, a street kid trying to survive in Night City who becomes a mercenary outlaw, the series manages to do something rare: it made fans love the Cyberpunk universe even more than the game.

The animation is kinetic and gorgeous. The story is compact and hits incredibly hard by the finale. This is peak Trigger ‚Äî the studio that made Kill la Kill and Gurren Lagann.

- **Genre**: Sci-fi action, cyberpunk
- **Episodes**: 10
- **Best for**: Fans of Cyberpunk 2077, fans of stylish action animation

#### 10. Gurren Lagann

**Gurren Lagann** is the most aggressively optimistic anime ever made. In a future where humanity lives underground, **Simon** and his brother-figure **Kamina** start a rebellion that escalates from digging tunnels to fighting in mechs to literally drilling through the universe.

The show is an unrelenting celebration of human potential, willpower, and the refusal to give up. If you want an anime that will make you feel genuinely pumped about being alive, Gurren Lagann is it.

- **Genre**: Mecha, action, adventure
- **Episodes**: 27
- **Best for**: Viewers who want high-energy, inspirational anime with heart

#### 16. Aggretsuko

**Aggretsuko** is a Netflix Original about a mild-mannered red panda named **Retsuko** who works an unfulfilling office job and secretly releases her rage by singing death metal at karaoke. It's both hilarious and surprisingly resonant.

The series has run for 5 seasons and covers topics like workplace stress, relationships, toxic bosses, social media, and career anxiety in ways that feel genuinely relatable.

- **Genre**: Comedy, slice of life, music
- **Episodes**: 50+
- **Best for**: Working adults who need to see their frustrations reflected back with humor
