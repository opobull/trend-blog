## üé¨ For All Ages: Family Classics

### 1. Night at the Museum (2006)

The new night watchman at New York's Museum of Natural History discovers that the exhibits come alive after dark ‚Äî including a T-Rex skeleton, Easter Island statues, Roman centurions, and a miniature civilization featuring cowboy Owen Wilson and Roman soldier Steve Carell.

**Night at the Museum** has been a family movie night staple for nearly 20 years for good reason: the premise never gets old, Ben Stiller's chaos-management is endlessly watchable, and there's always something happening on screen. The sequels (*Battle of the Smithsonian*, *Secret of the Tomb*) are also on Netflix.

- **Age**: 6+
- **Runtime**: 108 minutes
- **Best for**: First museum visit prep, kids who love history or dinosaurs

### 2. Mrs. Doubtfire (1993)

Robin Williams plays a devoted father who, after a messy divorce, disguises himself as a British nanny to spend time with his children. **Mrs. Doubtfire** is one of Williams' warmest performances ‚Äî genuinely funny, occasionally touching, and built on the kind of physical comedy that transcends generations.

Modern families note that the film's premise (deception as an expression of love) sparks interesting conversations with older kids about honesty, divorce, and family.

- **Age**: 8+
- **Runtime**: 125 minutes
- **Best for**: Rainy day family watch, Robin Williams fans of any age

### 3. The Sandlot (1993)

Summer 1962. Scotty Smalls is the new kid who can't play baseball ‚Äî until he's accepted by a group of neighborhood kids who play at the local sandlot. **The Sandlot** is a love letter to childhood summers, baseball, and friendship that somehow feels as fresh now as it did in 1993.

The Babe Ruth sequence, the dog (The Beast), and "You're killing me, Smalls" are embedded in American pop culture. Essential viewing for families before kids discover organized sports.

- **Age**: 8+
- **Runtime**: 101 minutes
- **Best for**: Baseball fans, summer viewing, nostalgia for simpler childhoods

### 4. Wonder (2017)

10-year-old Auggie Pullman (Jacob Tremblay) was born with a facial condition that required 27 surgeries. When he starts fifth grade for the first time ‚Äî having been homeschooled his whole life ‚Äî he navigates the social complexities of middle school while his family adjusts alongside him.

**Wonder** is exceptional family filmmaking: honest about how hard social integration can be, generous toward all its characters (including those who initially hurt Auggie), and genuinely moving without being manipulative. Julia Roberts and Owen Wilson are excellent as his parents.

- **Age**: 8+
- **Runtime**: 113 minutes
- **Best for**: Conversations about kindness, bullying, disability; families with kids entering new schools

## ü¶ï Animated Favorites

### 5. The Wild Robot (2024)

One of the finest animated films of recent years, now on Netflix. ROZZUM unit 7134 ("Roz") is stranded on a wild island after a shipwreck and must learn to survive by observing the animals around her. When she accidentally kills a goose couple and is left with their egg, she becomes an unlikely foster mother to the gosling Brightbill.

**The Wild Robot** is operating at Pixar's best level ‚Äî the animation is spectacular (mixing oil-painting textures with fluid movement), and the story hits genuine emotional depths about parenthood, belonging, and what it means to be "alive."

- **Age**: 6+
- **Runtime**: 102 minutes
- **Best for**: All ages ‚Äî genuinely moving for adults; funny and exciting for kids

### 6. The Mitchells vs. the Machines (2021)

A dysfunctional family on a road trip to college drop-off accidentally becomes humanity's last line of defense when the world's technology launches a robot uprising. Produced by Phil Lord and Christopher Miller (*Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse*, *The LEGO Movie*), **The Mitchells vs. the Machines** is fast, visually inventive, genuinely funny, and has real heart underneath the chaos.

The relationship between aspiring filmmaker Katie and her out-of-touch dad is unexpectedly touching. One of Netflix's best Original animated films.

- **Age**: 7+
- **Runtime**: 113 minutes
- **Best for**: Tech-loving families, teens, parents who want a film that actually speaks to them too

### 7. Wallace & Gromit: Vengeance Most Fowl (2024)

The beloved British inventor Wallace and his loyal beagle Gromit face their greatest challenge yet when Feathers McGraw ‚Äî the criminal penguin villain from *The Wrong Trousers* ‚Äî returns with a new scheme. ### 8. How to Train Your Dragon (2010)

Hiccup is a Viking teenager who befriends Toothless ‚Äî a rare Night Fury dragon ‚Äî and must choose between the traditions of his clan and what he knows to be right. **How to Train Your Dragon** is one of DreamWorks' absolute best: stunning flight sequences, genuine character development, and a message about overcoming fear and prejudice that doesn't feel preachy.

Both the original and the sequel (*How to Train Your Dragon 2*) recently arrived on Netflix ‚Äî perfect timing before the live-action remake.

- **Age**: 6+
- **Runtime**: 98 minutes
- **Best for**: Kids who love animals and adventure; dragon enthusiasts

### 9. Matilda the Musical (2022)

Roald Dahl's classic story of the extraordinary girl with telekinetic powers, adapted from the beloved stage musical. Emma Thompson is perfectly cast as the terrifying Miss Trunchbull; Alisha Weir is magnificent as Matilda. The musical numbers are genuinely great, and the film captures both the darkness and the triumph of Dahl's original story.

- **Age**: 7+
- **Runtime**: 117 minutes

### 10. Shrek (2001)

DreamWorks' enduring masterpiece. An antisocial ogre is sent to rescue a princess from a dragon-guarded castle ‚Äî and nothing goes as expected. **Shrek** works on multiple levels: silly humor for kids, fairy tale satire and pop culture jokes for adults, and genuine heart underneath the mud.

## How to Find More Family Content on Netflix

**Navigation tips**:
1. Browse to the "Kids" category in Netflix ‚Äî this gives age-filtered recommendations

**Kids Profiles on Netflix**: Creating a dedicated profile for your children automatically limits their access to age-appropriate content and shows recommendations tailored to kids.
