## 1. J. Cole Releases "The Fall-Off" - His Seventh and Final Album

![Entertainment Evening Roundup](/images/entertainment-evening-2026-02-06.jpg)

**J. Cole** has officially released **The Fall-Off**, his seventh studio album and what he intends to be his last. The double-disc project dropped on all streaming platforms on **February 6, 2026**, via Dreamville and Interscope Records.

The album brings Cole's journey full circle, connecting back to his 2007 debut mixtape *The Come Up*. In a message to fans, Cole explained the concept behind the two discs:

**Album Structure:**

| Disc | Title | Concept |
|------|-------|---------|
| Disc 1 | Disc 29 | Cole at age 29, returning home at a crossroads with love, craft, and city |
| Disc 2 | Disc 39 | Cole at age 39, older and closer to peace |

**Key Details:**

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| Release Date | February 6, 2026 |
| Format | Double Album (24 tracks) |
| Runtime | 1 hour 41 minutes |
| Label | Dreamville / Interscope |
| Executive Producers | Cole, Ibrahim Hamad, T-Minus |

The album's teaser featured narration from comedian Dan Harumi, whose May 2025 YouTube video "How Long Is America Supposed to Last?" explores themes of fame and inevitable decline—fitting perfectly with Cole's meditations on legacy.

## 2. Steve Carell's "Rooster" Sets March 8 Premiere on HBO Max with New Trailer

**HBO Max** has released the first trailer and premiere date for **Rooster**, a new comedy series starring **Steve Carell**. The show premieres on **March 8, 2026**, with 10 episodes releasing weekly through May 10.

Created by **Bill Lawrence** (Ted Lasso, Shrinking, Bad Monkey) and **Matt Tarses**, Rooster follows an author whose best-selling books feature a character nicknamed "Rooster." When his daughter, a college professor, faces a personal crisis, he visits campus to help—only to get entangled in academic drama.

**Plot Points from Trailer:**
- Carell's daughter (Charly Clive) has relationship problems after her husband (Phil Dunster) leaves her for a graduate student (Lauren Tsai)
- She "accidentally" burned down the faculty house
- She "purposefully" punched her estranged husband

**Key Information:**

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| Platform | HBO Max |
| Premiere Date | March 8, 2026 |
| Episodes | 10 |
| Schedule | Weekly (through May 10) |
| Showrunners | Bill Lawrence & Matt Tarses |

**Cast:** Steve Carell, Charly Clive, Danielle Deadwyler, Phil Dunster, John C. McGinley, Lauren Tsai, Rory Scovel, Connie Britton

The series reunites Lawrence with John C. McGinley, who will also return as Dr. Cox in the upcoming *Scrubs* revival.

## 3. Netflix Unveils "BAKI-DOU: The Invincible Samurai" English Dub Trailer

**Netflix** has released a new trailer for **BAKI-DOU: The Invincible Samurai**, the latest anime installment in the beloved martial arts franchise. All 13 episodes will premiere globally on **February 26, 2026**.

The series continues the story of **Baki Hanma** as he faces his most legendary opponent yet: the resurrected samurai **Miyamoto Musashi**, considered the greatest swordsman in Japanese history.

**Key Information:**

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| Platform | Netflix |
| Release Date | February 26, 2026 |
| Episodes | 13 |
| Audio | Japanese & English dub |
| Studio | TMS Entertainment |

The trailer promises a "battle of eras"—pitting modern mixed martial arts against the lethal techniques of feudal Japan's greatest warrior. The English dub cast includes notable voice actors, maintaining the high production values fans expect from the Baki franchise.

This marks the fourth Baki anime adaptation on Netflix, following *Baki* (2018), *Baki Hanma* (2021), and *Baki Hanma: Son of Ogre* (2023).

## 4. Kelly Rowland & Method Man Star in "Relationship Goals" on Prime Video

**Prime Video's** new romantic comedy **Relationship Goals** premiered on **February 4, 2026**, starring **Kelly Rowland** and **Cliff "Method Man" Smith** in a workplace romance that's heating up streaming charts.

Based on the New York Times bestseller by Pastor Mike Todd, the film follows **Leah Caldwell** (Rowland), a high-flying television producer on track to become the first woman to lead New York's top morning show. Her path collides with **Jarrett Roy** (Method Man), brought in as her competition for the same position.

**Key Information:**

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| Platform | Prime Video |
| Release Date | February 4, 2026 |
| Director | Linda Mendoza |
| Based On | "Relationship Goals" by Pastor Mike Todd |
| Genre | Romantic Comedy |

**Cast:** Kelly Rowland, Method Man, Robin Thede, Annie Gonzalez, Dennis Haysbert, Matt Walsh

The film held its NYC premiere on February 3, with Rowland and Method Man promoting their on-screen chemistry. Pastor Mike Todd, whose book inspired the film, celebrated seeing his vision come to life: "This is a product of me believing in a vision when nobody believed in it."
